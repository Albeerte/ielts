Reading Passage 1: Urban Gardens

A In many large cities, unused land has been converted into community gardens. These spaces are usually managed by local residents, who grow vegetables, herbs, and flowers together.

B Supporters claim that urban gardens improve public health because they encourage people to spend time outdoors and eat fresh food. They may also strengthen social ties by giving neighbours a shared project.

C However, not every garden succeeds. Some projects struggle when volunteers move away or when landowners decide to sell the site. Long-term agreements and clear leadership are often needed.

D Researchers have found that the best gardens combine practical food production with education. Workshops on composting, water saving, and seasonal planting can make the garden useful even for people who do not have their own plot.
