The Future of Artificial Intelligence in Education

Reading Passage

A Artificial intelligence is becoming a familiar presence in education. In many schools and universities, software can now recommend exercises, check simple answers, and identify topics that students may need to review. Supporters argue that these tools can help teachers respond to learners more quickly and with greater precision.

B One of the most common uses of AI is adaptive learning. Instead of giving every student the same sequence of tasks, an adaptive platform changes the level and order of activities according to the learner's performance. A student who answers vocabulary questions easily may move on to more complex reading tasks, while another student may receive extra practice first.

C However, researchers warn that AI should not replace teachers. Human teachers notice motivation, confidence, classroom behaviour, and emotional needs in ways that data systems often cannot. For this reason, many experts believe the best future classroom will combine intelligent software with professional human judgement.

D Privacy is another important issue. Educational platforms may collect information about mistakes, study habits, and even the speed at which a student reads or types. Schools must decide who can access this information and how long it should be stored. Without clear rules, useful learning data can become a source of risk.

E Despite these concerns, AI may make education more accessible. Automated captions, speech recognition, and translation tools can support students who previously found lessons difficult to follow. If used carefully, artificial intelligence may not make learning less human; it may give teachers more time for the human parts of teaching.
